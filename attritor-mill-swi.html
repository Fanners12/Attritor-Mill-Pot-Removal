ERT — Controlled procedure — Air-reactive material
Standard work instruction
Attritor mill — reactive powder disposal
Stuck pot removal, submersion & ball recovery
Performed by
ERT team (2-person min.)
Scope
Attritor mills — all units
Hazard
Air-reactive powder — fire/explosion risk
Duration
Day 1 + Day 3 return visit
⚠️
Critical hazard: The pot has no lid when removed from the mill. The powder is immediately exposed to air the moment the pot leaves the mill. Oxidation, fire, or deflagration can occur within seconds. Transport to the water bin must be immediate and without any stops or delays. Two ERT personnel must be present at all times — no exceptions.
Overall progress — 0 of 16 steps complete
Phase 1 — Day 1 Pot removal, submersion & agitation ▾
Required PPE — don before leaving prep area
🦺 ERT suit & hood
🥽 Full face shield
🧤 Fire-proof ERT gloves
👥 2-person rule — never alone
1
Confirm both ERT team members are present, fully suited, and signed in before approaching the mill.
2
Position the water bin directly outside the building exit nearest to the attritor mill before approaching the mill. Confirm the bin is full of water.
Water level must be sufficient to fully submerge the pot. Verify this before touching the mill. The route from mill to bin must be clear and unobstructed.
3
Shut down and isolate the attritor mill following the standard lockout/tagout procedure. Confirm the mill has stopped completely before touching the pot.
⚡ Steps 4 and 5 must be completed together without any pause. The pot has no lid — powder is open to air as soon as it leaves the mill.
4
Carefully remove the pot from the mill. Keep it upright and level at all times — do not tilt or tip. One operator holds the pot; the second operator immediately moves ahead to hold the exit door open.
⚠ The pot is open — powder is exposed to air from this moment. Move directly to the bin without stopping.
5
Carry the pot in a single direct movement to the water bin outside. Lower the pot into the water immediately — do not set it down anywhere en route.
⚠ Lower slowly and steadily to avoid splashing. Submerge fully before releasing.
6
Once the pot is fully submerged, agitate the contents using a long-handled tool. Stir vigorously so all powder and milling balls are completely wetted throughout.
Continue agitation for a minimum of 2–3 minutes. Ensure no dry powder remains visible anywhere in the pot or on the bin floor.
7
Confirm pot remains fully submerged. Secure or cover the bin to prevent access by unauthorised personnel.
8
Record the date and time of submersion in the sign-off section. Leave the pot undisturbed for a minimum of 72 hours.
Phase 2 — Day 3 Pot retrieval, emptying & ball recovery ▾
Required PPE — don before approaching the bin
🦺 ERT suit & hood
🥽 Full face shield
🧤 Fire-proof ERT gloves
👥 2-person rule — never alone
1
Confirm a minimum of 72 hours have elapsed since submersion. Check the Day 1 timestamp recorded in the sign-off section before proceeding.
2
Both ERT team members to don full PPE as listed above before approaching the bin.
3
Position the mesh/sieve receptacle securely beside the water bin, ready to receive the pot contents.
4
Retrieve the pot from the water bin. Keep it low and stable — do not raise above waist height during transfer to the sieve.
5
Tip the pot contents — slurry and milling balls — into the mesh/sieve. Allow the slurry to drain through fully. Do not rush.
Product slurry drains through the mesh. Milling balls are retained for collection.
6
Collect the milling balls from the sieve. Count must match the pre-run ball count recorded in the mill logbook before the pot is signed off.
7
Dispose of slurry water and residual product in accordance with the site waste disposal procedure for reactive materials.
8
Clean the pot and bin area. Return all equipment to designated storage. Remove and decontaminate PPE as per ERT decon procedure.
